Critical advice
01
PYQ practice is non-negotiable. TU boards are heavily pattern-based. Solve at least 5 years of past papers per subject.
02
Write C code on paper. The boards are handwritten. Practising only on a computer will not prepare you.
03
Don't leave Maths for week 4. It needs daily practice. 30 minutes every day beats a 3-hour cram session.
04
Fundamentals = easy marks. Mostly conceptual — one thorough read and a revision is enough for a strong score.
05
Sleep is not optional. Studying 6 hrs sleep-deprived is worse than 4 hrs well-rested. Protect 7–8 hrs in week 4.
